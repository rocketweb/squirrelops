# Drill Layer

The docs in the parent directory are knowledge. This directory is practice. Knowledge gets you ready to read about the product. Practice gets you ready to talk about it live, under pressure, without notes. They're different skills, and the second one is the one that closes.

The honest problem this solves: you can read all twelve enablement docs and still freeze when a skeptic interrupts you mid-sentence with "98.5% of what?" Reading builds recognition. Live calls need retrieval and delivery. This layer trains those.

## What's here

- **`cheat-sheets/`**: ultra-terse glance cards. The 30-seconds-before-a-call reference. Master card plus numbers, competitive, pricing, personas, framework, objections.
- **`flash-cards.tsv`** + **`flash-cards.md`**: 94 active-recall cards for Anki. The daily retrieval engine. Import instructions in the `.md`.
- **`mock-dialogues/`**: six realistic call transcripts modeling good handling, including the stumble-and-recover moments. The flow-and-rhythm trainer.

## The three skills and the tool for each

| Skill | What it means | Tool |
|---|---|---|
| **Recall** | The fact is there when you reach for it | Flashcards (daily) |
| **Delivery** | You say it in two sentences, not five | Mock dialogues (read aloud), recording yourself |
| **Composure** | You hold the line when pushed | Mock dialogue 06, drilled |

Notebook LM (see `../NOTEBOOK-LM-GUIDE.md`) handles a fourth, **priming**, the passive warm-up. Priming is real but it's the easy 20%. This layer is the hard 80%.

## The daily loop (15 minutes)

1. **Flashcards, 10 minutes.** Do Anki's scheduled review. Answer every card OUT LOUD before flipping. Grade honestly; hesitation means "Again." Speaking is the rep that transfers.
2. **One dialogue, 5 minutes.** Read one mock dialogue, but read Matt's lines aloud as if they're yours, pausing where he pauses. Rotate through all six over the week. Dialogue 06 (hostile numbers) gets read twice as often as the others.

## The weekly loop (once)

1. **Record yourself.** Phone, no notes. Do the four confidence checkpoints below in one sitting.
2. **Listen back.** Mark every stumble, every ramble past two sentences, every number you fudged.
3. **Convert stumbles to drills.** Whatever you fumbled becomes next week's focus: the relevant flashcards get extra reps, the relevant dialogue gets re-read.

## The confidence checkpoints (the bar)

You are ready for a live call when you can do these recorded, cold, no notes:

1. The 30-second pitch, clean, one take.
2. The 98.5% denominator explanation, volunteering the 76% before being asked.
3. Three objections in under 20 seconds each: "why not Lakera," "you have no customers," "98.5% of what."
4. Your top three honest limits, stated without flinching: pre-customer, not certified, two modules are foundations.

If all four are smooth, you're ready. "I read the docs" is not the bar. "I recorded myself and didn't stumble" is.

## The order to build the habit

Week 1: flashcards daily, nothing else. Get recall solid first.
Week 2: add one dialogue a day, read aloud.
Week 3: add the weekly recording. Find your stumbles.
Week 4: you should clear the four checkpoints. If not, the recordings tell you exactly which cards and dialogues to repeat.

## Why this is structured this way

The phase-1 assessment of this corpus was blunt: it was an encyclopedia, not a practice system. Notebook LM listening is passive, and passive doesn't encode fluency. The fix is retrieval under simulated pressure: flashcards for recall, dialogues for delivery and composure, recordings for honest feedback. That's what this layer is. The knowledge was necessary. It was not sufficient. This is the sufficient part.

## Maintenance

When the product or the numbers change, update the source docs first, then propagate here: the flashcard TSV, the relevant cheat sheet, and any dialogue that quotes the changed fact. The drill material is downstream of the knowledge docs. Keep it in sync or you'll drill yourself into a stale answer.
